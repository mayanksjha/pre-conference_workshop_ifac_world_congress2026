Workshop abstract

Scientific rationale

Industrial and mission-critical systems increasingly operate in closed-loop settings where safety, resilience, and adaptability are indispensable. This workshop focuses on the convergence of fault-resilient control, health-aware control, prognostics, and safe learning for safety-critical systems. It aims to bridge model-based, data-driven, and hybrid methods that account explicitly for degradation, uncertainty, faults, and distribution shifts.

The workshop brings together researchers working on state and health estimation, prognostics-in-the-loop control, learning-enabled decision-making, observer design, anomaly detection, digital twins, and resilient control. The broader objective is to connect methodological advances with deployment needs in aerospace, transportation, energy systems, robotics, manufacturing, and critical infrastructure.

Workshop objectives

  • Connect diagnosis, prognostics, and health information with closed-loop control.
  • Advance safe learning and resilient autonomy for safety-critical applications.
  • Discuss hybrid model-based and data-driven tools for dependable operation.
  • Highlight digital twins and deployable methods for real industrial systems.
Themes

Core technical topics

The workshop spans monitoring, prognostics, control, learning, and digital-twin-enabled decision support for critical engineering systems.

Health-aware control

Embedding state of health, degradation, reliability, and remaining useful life into closed-loop decision-making.

Safe learning

Safe reinforcement learning, control barrier functions, safe exploration, and safety filters for autonomy.

Fault-resilient control

Fault diagnosis, fault-tolerant control, adaptive reconfiguration, and resilient operation under abnormal conditions.

Operational digital twins

Hybrid physics/AI models, twin-enabled inference, predictive monitoring, and decision support for complex assets.

Application domains

Aerospace, mobility, energy, robotics, infrastructure, and smart industrial systems.

Programme structure

Workshop flow

The programme is organized around invited talks, technical discussions, and cross-cutting dialogue on safe and health-aware control.

01

Opening session

Workshop motivation, technical scope, and positioning relative to IFAC themes on safety, resilience, PHM, and learning.

02

Foundations: HAC, diagnosis, and prognostics

Methods that integrate state of health, degradation indicators, and prognostic information into monitoring and control.
