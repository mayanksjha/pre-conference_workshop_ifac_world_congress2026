Workshop abstract

Scientific rationale

Industrial and mission-critical systems increasingly operate in closed-loop settings where safety, resilience, and adaptability are indispensable. This proposed workshop focuses on the convergence of fault-resilient control, health-aware control, prognostics, and safe learning for safety-critical systems. It aims to bridge model-based, data-driven, and hybrid methods that account explicitly for degradation, uncertainty, faults, and distribution shifts.

The workshop is designed to bring together researchers working on state and health estimation, prognostics-in-the-loop control, robust and learning-based model predictive control, observer design, anomaly detection, digital twins, and resilient multi-agent systems. The broader objective is to connect methodological advances with deployment needs in aerospace, transportation, energy systems, robotics, manufacturing, and critical infrastructure.

Themes

Core technical topics

The site is framed around the public workshop-track theme and the IFAC working-group agenda on health-aware control and safe learning for safety-critical systems.

Health-aware control

Embedding state of health, degradation, reliability, and remaining useful life into closed-loop decision-making.

Safe learning

Safe reinforcement learning, control barrier functions, safe exploration, and safety filters for autonomy.

Fault-resilient control

Fault diagnosis, fault-tolerant control, adaptive reconfiguration, and resilient operation under abnormal conditions.

Operational digital twins

Hybrid physics/AI models, twin-enabled inference, predictive monitoring, and decision support for complex assets.

Robust & learning-based MPC

Robust, stochastic, and data-driven predictive control methods that remain dependable under uncertainty.

Observers & estimation

State estimation, interval observers, anomaly detection, health indicators, and uncertainty quantification.

Distributed resilience

Networked, multi-agent, and cyber-physical systems that require scalable safety and resilient coordination.

Application domains

Aerospace, mobility, energy, robotics, infrastructure, and smart industrial systems.

Illustrative workshop flow

Opening framing session
